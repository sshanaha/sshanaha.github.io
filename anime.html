HISTORY

Anime originated in Japan and combines art, storytelling, and animation. It began in the early 20th century, inspired by Western animation.

Timeline

  • 1
    Early Days (1917-1950s):

    The first known Japanese animated film, Namakura Gatana (1917), was a short silent animation. Post-WWII, Osamu Tezuka, often called the "God of Manga," introduced Astro Boy (1963), marking anime's modern era.

  • 2
    Golden Age (1970s-1980s):

    The 70s saw mecha anime rise (Mobile Suit Gundam). By the 80s, anime like Dragon Ball and Akira gained international acclaim, showing mature and diverse storytelling.

  • 3
    Global Popularity (1990s-Present):

    The 90s brought classics like Sailor Moon, Pok√©mon, and Neon Genesis Evangelion. Today, anime spans genres and enjoys a massive global audience, with streaming services making it more accessible than ever.
