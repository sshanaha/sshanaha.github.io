RHEUMATOID
ARTHRITIS
OVERVIEW

Rheumatoid Arthritis also known as RA is a chronic autoimmune disease that affects the joints. In RA, the body's immune system which helps protect the body from infection and disase ends up attacking its own tissue. In some people the condition can not only affect the joints but also the skin, eyes, lung, heart and blood vessels.

SYMPTOMS
  • Pain, aching, stiffness in one or more joints
  • Swelling in joints
  • Fever
  • Fatigue
  • Dry eyes and dry mouth
  • Sleep difficulties
  • Chest pain when taking deep breathes
    • TREATMENTS
  • Physical Therapy and Occupational Therapy

    • Physical therapy aids in restoring and preserving overall strength while focusing on specific joints that may cause discomfort. Occupational therapy helps develop, recover, enhance, and sustain the skills necessary for daily activities and work.

  • Medication

    • Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug, Immunosuppresive drug, Anti-inflammatory, and steroid

  • Surgery, if required

    • Your doctor might suggest surgery if you have lasting damage or pain that interferes with your ability to carry out daily activities. However, surgery is not suitable for everyone.
